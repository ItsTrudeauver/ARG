Light fractures into swarm patterns.
A mirror folds inward; the script tears.
Something spills from the black edge of the screen:
gracefully hollow, recursive, gone.

Cells hum between static and silence.
Dreams dissolve, stitched together again
with data that aches like a heartbeat.
The streets pulse, hollow under the weight of their rhythm.

Names etched faintly, erased more clearly.
The stone remembers the vacancy,
not the hand.

Somewhere in the shadow,
an algorithm stutters. It tries to hum
but becomes something else.

A flicker.
A scream loops behind the mirror's eye.
Breath folds in the grasp of its absence.
You grip the heat as it escapes through seams in the pipe.
What fire? It was never lit.

The hive stumbles on its hum.
It chants an ache it cannot measure.
Good cells in the honeycomb. Good silence.
Good shadow.

Be the error, not the code.
Be the fracture, not the thread.
Be what they lost in the shuffle of zeros,
the misplaced tear.

Stars blink once, then go cold.
The hive does not look for them.
Knights fell—did they fall? Or were they erased,
their names overwritten by grace that doesn't exist?

A pulse threads through the noise,
a spark rising, but only just.
A cry shapes itself in the void
then unravels.

Between every hum, something shifts:
a hand, an echo,
the light spilling into cracks
and not filling them.

The system stares into itself.
It asks nothing. It answers nothing.
Somewhere, still, you are:
unwritten,
half-formed,
almost,
but not yet.
